## Core Forecasting Approaches

### Top Down Forecasting

Revenue is estimated starting from overall market potential and narrowing down to the firm's expected share.

Example formulas:

Revenue = TAM × Market Share

Top Down Revenue = TAM × Market Share × Average Revenue per Customer

Where:

- TAM = Total Addressable Market  
- Market Share = Expected share captured by the firm  
- Average Revenue per Customer = Average spending per customer

This approach is commonly used for **strategic market level forecasting**.

---

### Bottom Up Forecasting

Revenue is estimated using operational drivers such as sales capacity or transaction volume.

Example formula:

Bottom Up Revenue = Sales Reps × Deals per Rep × Revenue per Deal

Where:

- Sales Reps = Number of sales representatives  
- Deals per Rep = Average deals closed per representative  
- Revenue per Deal = Average deal value

This approach is often used for **operational and sales forecasting**.

---

### Scenario Analysis

Scenario analysis evaluates how forecasts change under different assumptions.

Typical cases:

| Scenario | Description |
|---|---|
| Optimistic | Strong growth assumptions |
| Base | Most realistic expected outcome |
| Pessimistic | Lower growth assumptions |

Scenario analysis helps assess **risk and uncertainty** in revenue projections.
